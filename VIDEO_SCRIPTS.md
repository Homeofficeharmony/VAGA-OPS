# Stealth Reset — 60-Second Video Scripts
## Nervous System Ops Pack · Production-Ready

**Format notes:** Film in portrait (9:16) and landscape (16:9). Clean background — dark wall or desk setup. Minimal wardrobe. No music underneath — silence is the aesthetic. Captions on. Tone: precise, calm authority. Not hushed wellness. Not aggressive coaching. Think: surgeon explaining a procedure.

## Script 01 — The Ear-Apex Pull
### *For: Frozen / Shutdown State*

**[0:00 – 0:05]**
*(Direct to camera. Still. Minimal movement.)*

"You're frozen. That's a biological state, not a character flaw. Here's the 60-second way out."

---

**[0:05 – 0:12]**
*(Demonstrate hand position — reach both hands up, thumbs and index fingers gripping the uppermost ridge of each ear.)*

"Reach up. Find the apex — the very top ridge of your ear. Grip it between thumb and index finger. Both sides simultaneously."

---

**[0:12 – 0:28]**
*(Hold the pull. Maintain steady tension upward and slightly outward. Head is still.)*

"Pull gently upward and outward. Not hard — firm and steady. You're looking for a subtle stretch through the jaw, behind the ears, into the base of the skull. Hold it there."

---

**[0:28 – 0:42]**
*(Begin slow head rotations — 20 degrees left, pause, 20 degrees right. Maintain the ear pull throughout.)*

"Without releasing the grip — slowly rotate your head left. Pause. Now right. Just twenty degrees each direction. You may feel a pull behind your eyes. That's correct."

---

**[0:42 – 0:54]**
*(Release hands. Hands rest in lap. Eyes close briefly.)*

"Release. Rest your hands in your lap. Notice the warmth spreading across your face and neck. That's blood returning to previously restricted tissue."

---

**[0:54 – 1:00]**
*(Direct to camera. One slow nasal breath visible.)*

"Inhale through your nose. Hold for two counts. Out through your mouth. You've just activated your vagus nerve via the auricular branch. Name your next single action. Do only that."

---

**[END CARD — 3 seconds]**

## Script 02 — The Rib-Cage Expansion
### *For: Anxious / High-Alert State*

**[0:00 – 0:05]**
*(Seated. Hands visible in lap. Direct camera.)*

"Your nervous system is in fight-or-flight. You're breathing shallow. This resets it in sixty seconds — physiologically, not philosophically."

---

**[0:05 – 0:15]**
*(Place both palms flat on lower ribs, fingers angled inward toward the sternum. Visible contact with the body.)*

"Both palms on your lower ribs. Fingers pointing inward. Feel the structure of the rib cage beneath your hands. This is your baseline — notice how little movement there is right now."

---

**[0:15 – 0:32]**
*(Execute first full cycle — visible lateral rib expansion on inhale, shoulders staying down. Slow audible exhale through pursed lips.)*

"Inhale for four counts — expand the ribs sideways into your palms. Not up into your shoulders. Out to the sides. One... two... three... four.

Now exhale for eight counts through pursed lips. Slow. Controlled. Feel the ribs compress back inward."

---

**[0:32 – 0:48]**
*(Second full cycle. On exhale, jaw drops and eyes soften visibly.)*

"Again. Inhale — four counts, ribs wide. And out — eight counts.

On this exhale: let your jaw go loose. Unclench. And consciously soften your eyes — unfocus them. You're not analyzing anything right now."

---

**[0:48 – 0:56]**
*(Hands lower into lap. Normal breath returns. Brief pause.)*

"Return to natural breath. The extended exhale you just did activated your baroreceptors — those are pressure sensors in your blood vessels that spike parasympathetic tone. That chest tightness? It's releasing."

---

**[0:56 – 1:00]**
*(Direct camera. Still.)*

"Pick one task. Set a twenty-five minute timer. Close everything else."

---

**[END CARD — 3 seconds]**
`RIB-CAGE EXPANSION · RESPIRATORY RESET · 60 SECONDS`
`Nervous System Ops Pack`

## Script 03 — The Peripheral Vision Soften
### *For: Safe / Flow State Calibration*

**[0:00 – 0:05]**
*(Relaxed posture. Slight smile — not forced. Not performing calm, embodying it.)*

"You're close to flow. This protocol locks you in. It takes sixty seconds and uses your visual system to quiet your threat-detection circuitry."

---

**[0:05 – 0:18]**
*(Eyes fix on a point slightly above the camera lens — direct, soft. Not a stare.)*

"Fix your gaze on a point roughly two meters ahead at eye level. Soft focus — you're not analyzing it, just resting on it. Now — without moving your eyes — expand your awareness outward."

---

**[0:18 – 0:38]**
*(Eyes remain forward but clearly taking in the periphery — subtle shift in quality of attention visible.)*

"Notice color at the edges of your visual field. Movement in your periphery. The width of the room entering your awareness. This is called panoramic vision — or open monitoring — and it's the opposite of the tunnel focus that anxiety creates.

Hold this wide-angle awareness."

---

**[0:38 – 0:50]**
*(Jaw visibly releases. Shoulders drop subtly.)*

"As you hold it — let your jaw unclench. Drop your shoulders two centimeters. Not a big movement. Just gravity doing its job.

Notice: your thoughts are slower. That's the superior colliculus damping your amygdala's threat signal. This isn't a metaphor. It's architecture."

---

**[0:50 – 1:00]**
*(Slow nasal inhale. Full exhale through open mouth — audible "hahhh.")*

"Slow nasal inhale. And out through your open mouth — hahhh.

You're in the room. You're safe. You have ninety minutes. Go build something."

---

**[END CARD — 3 seconds]**
`PERIPHERAL VISION SOFTEN · OPTIC REGULATION · 60 SECONDS`
`Nervous System Ops Pack`

## Production Notes

**Camera:** Eye-level or slightly below. Never above — removes authority.
**Lighting:** Key light at 45° from camera left. No ring light (too soft).
**Audio:** Lavalier or high-quality condenser. Room tone should be near-silent.
**Pacing:** Slower than you think. The pauses are the content.
**Captions:** Always. White on dark background. Font: monospace.
**B-roll:** None needed for first release. Let the stillness hold.
