# The Rupture Protocol — Burnout SOP
## For Solopreneurs at Level 8–10 Burnout
### Nervous System Ops Pack · Decision Logic

---

> **Definition of Rupture:** A rupture is not a bad day. It is a systemic breakdown where the nervous system's capacity to regulate has been exceeded. Productivity tactics will not fix it. This SOP won't fix it in an afternoon. It is a protocol for moving through it with minimal additional damage.

## TIER 1 — Pre-Rupture (Score 6–7)
### *Objective: Stop the slide before it becomes a collapse.*

**Step 1 — HALT Check (immediate, 2 min)**
Before anything else, assess the four physiological accelerants:
- **H**ungry? → Eat. Not optimize. Eat.
- **A**ngry/Anxious? → That's the state. Note it.
- **L**onely? → When did you last have real human contact?
- **T**ired? → Sleep debt compounds everything.

If 2 or more are YES → your baseline is compromised before any other variable.

**Step 2 — Stop the Bleeding (5 min)**
- Close all browser tabs.
- Set phone to Do Not Disturb, physical face-down.
- Tell Slack/calendar you're unavailable for the next 2 hours.
- Do NOT send emails, messages, or make decisions right now.

*You are in a cognitively impaired state. Decisions made at 6–7 will likely need to be walked back.*

**Step 3 — Run a Reset Protocol (60–120 sec)**
- Open Regulation Station Dashboard
- Select "Anxious/High-Alert" (even if it feels like more — this is the right entry point for 6–7)
- Run the Rib-Cage Expansion protocol
- Do not skip this. The protocol is not optional.

**Step 4 — One Task Rule**
- From the Task Filter, select exactly ONE low-intensity task
- Complete it
- Log the session

**Step 5 — End-of-Day Non-Negotiables**
- Eat a real meal (not at desk)
- Sleep before midnight
- Schedule one recovery activity tomorrow morning (walk, not gym)

**Decision Gate — after Tier 1:**
```
Did symptoms improve after 24 hours with Tier 1?
├── YES → Return to normal Regulation Station use. Monitor.
└── NO  → Move to TIER 2.
```

## TIER 2 — Rupture Response (Score 8–10)
### *Objective: Contain, not perform.*

> **Critical Rule:** At Level 8+, the goal is zero net damage — not productivity. Any revenue you "push through" at this level will cost you 3x in recovery time and quality.

**Step 1 — Declare the Rupture (private, 2 min)**
Write in your log or a private note:
> *"I am in rupture. I am temporarily operating above capacity. I am not broken. This is a system overload, not a character verdict."*

This is not journaling. It is nervous system narrative — you are giving your threat-detection circuitry a frame that does not trigger shame spiral, which would compound the rupture.

**Step 2 — Contact Your Support System (same day)**
You must contact at least ONE person who knows your situation:
- Business coach or peer
- Therapist or somatic practitioner
- Trusted friend who understands solopreneurship
- Mastermind group member

**What to say:**
> *"I've hit a rupture. I'm not looking for advice right now — I just need you to know I'm in it."*

Isolation during rupture is the leading accelerant. Break it within 24 hours.

**Step 3 — Business Triage (30 min, one time)**
Identify the three categories:

| Category | Items | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **On Fire** | Things that will have immediate consequences if ignored | Delegate, delay, or communicate |
| **Smoldering** | Things that need attention but have margin | Reschedule with buffer |
| **Can Wait** | Everything else | Put in a parking lot doc. Do not look at it again until recovery. |

*Do not try to solve the "On Fire" category today. Communicate to relevant parties that there will be a delay. Most things that feel like fire are not.*

**Step 4 — The 72-Hour Minimal Ops Protocol**
For the next 3 days:
- Work maximum 4 hours per day
- No meetings with clients if avoidable (reschedule with 48h notice)
- No new pitches or proposals
- No major financial decisions
- 8+ hours sleep, non-negotiable
- One walk outside per day, no phone

**What you CAN do in 72 hours:**
- Fulfil existing deliverables that are nearly complete
- Send brief update communications
- Administrative tasks (filing, invoicing already-completed work)
- Rest without guilt

**Step 5 — Root Cause Identification (day 3 or 4)**
Once acute symptoms have reduced to a 5 or below, identify the rupture source:

```
What caused this rupture?

├── WORKLOAD OVERLOAD
│   └── → Review your capacity model. How many clients/projects = sustainable?
│         You're currently above it. Something must come out.
│
├── FINANCIAL STRESS
│   └── → This is not a hustle problem. This is a business model problem.
│         What is the single lever that would reduce financial pressure fastest?
│         Identify it. Do only that.
│
├── ISOLATION / LONELINESS
│   └── → You are running a solo operation without a social co-regulation network.
│         This is physiologically unsustainable long-term.
│         What is your weekly minimum human contact threshold?
│
├── IDENTITY / MEANING RUPTURE
│   └── → You've lost the "why." Or the "why" is no longer true.
│         This requires reflection, not tactics. Schedule time with a coach.
│
└── COMPOUNDING FACTORS
    └── → Multiple of the above. Do not try to solve all simultaneously.
          Rank by which is causing the most physiological dysregulation.
          Address one.
```

**Decision Gate — after 72 hours:**
```
Score after Tier 2 protocol?
├── 5 or below → Return to Tier 1, then Regulation Station. You're through the acute phase.
├── Still 8–10 → Move to TIER 3.
```

## TIER 3 — Sustained Rupture (Score 8–10 after 72+ hours)
### *Objective: Prevent burnout from becoming a health event.*

If you are still at 8–10 after properly executing Tier 1 and 2, this is no longer a productivity problem. It is a health situation.

**Mandatory actions:**

**1. See a professional within 5 business days**
- GP / physician for physical assessment
- Therapist, somatic practitioner, or psychiatrist
- This is not optional. Sustained HPA-axis overactivation causes measurable cortisol damage. This is a medical situation.

**2. Revenue Protection**
- Notify one trusted client that you are managing a health situation and will need adjusted timelines
- Do not disclose more than necessary
- Most clients will respond with more grace than your nervous system predicts

**3. Remove the largest stressor temporarily**
Identify the single largest daily stressor in your business. Can any version of it be paused for 14 days?
- Pause a service offering
- Push back a launch
- Hire a contractor for one task

The goal is to create 20% more capacity buffer. That buffer is where recovery happens.

**4. Daily Minimum Protocol (Tier 3)**
- Regulation Station check-in: morning only
- One Stealth Reset before noon
- 20-minute walk: non-negotiable
- No work after 7 PM
- No news, social media, or financial content after 6 PM

## Recovery Timeline — What to Expect

| Phase | Duration | Signs |
|---|---|---|
| **Acute** | 1–3 days | Score 8–10, physical symptoms, cognitive fog |
| **Sub-acute** | 4–7 days | Score 5–7, inconsistent capacity, emotional volatility |
| **Repair** | 1–3 weeks | Score 3–5, returning function, better sleep |
| **Rebuild** | 1–3 months | Score 1–3, proactive capacity, system redesign |

*You cannot rush these phases. Attempting to "perform recovery" — doing all the right things but without genuine rest — extends each phase.*

---

## Prevention Protocols (Post-Recovery)

Once you are below a 4, install these as operating standards:

**Weekly:**
- Review State Log from the past 7 days. If 3+ days show Score 6+, treat as pre-rupture.
- One full offline day (no client communication)

**Monthly:**
- Capacity audit: active clients × avg hours/week. Is it above your sustainable ceiling?
- Revenue runway check: do you have 60+ days runway? Below this = chronic sympathetic activation.

**Quarterly:**
- Full SOP review: what would I delegate if I had 20% more budget?
- Relationship audit: who in your life co-regulates your nervous system? Are you seeing them enough?
